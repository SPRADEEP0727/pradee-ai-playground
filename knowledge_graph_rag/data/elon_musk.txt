Elon Reeve Musk was born on June 28, 1971, in Pretoria, South Africa. His mother, Maye Musk, is a Canadian-South African model and dietitian. His father, Errol Musk, is a South African electromechanical engineer. Elon has a younger brother, Kimbal Musk, and a younger sister, Tosca Musk.

As a child, Elon was an avid reader and taught himself computer programming. At the age of 12, he created a video game called Blastar and sold the source code to a magazine called PC and Office Technology for approximately $500.

At age 17, Musk moved to Canada to attend Queen's University in Kingston, Ontario. He later transferred to the University of Pennsylvania, where he earned two bachelor's degrees: one in Economics from the Wharton School and one in Physics from the College of Arts and Sciences.

In 1995, Musk moved to Stanford University in California to pursue a PhD in energy physics but dropped out after just two days to pursue entrepreneurship during the internet boom.

In 1995, Elon Musk and his brother Kimbal Musk co-founded Zip2, a city guide software company. Compaq acquired Zip2 in 1999 for approximately $307 million. Elon received $22 million from the sale.

In 1999, Musk co-founded X.com, an online financial services and payment company. X.com merged with Confinity in 2000, which had developed a money transfer service called PayPal. The merged company focused on PayPal and was renamed PayPal in 2001. eBay acquired PayPal in 2002 for $1.5 billion. Musk, as the largest shareholder, received $165 million from the sale.

In 2002, Elon Musk founded Space Exploration Technologies Corp, known as SpaceX, with the goal of reducing space transportation costs and enabling the colonization of Mars. SpaceX is headquartered in Hawthorne, California. The company developed the Falcon 1, Falcon 9, and Falcon Heavy rockets. SpaceX also developed the Dragon spacecraft for cargo and crew missions to the International Space Station. In 2020, SpaceX's Crew Dragon became the first commercial spacecraft to carry astronauts to the ISS, partnering with NASA. SpaceX is also developing Starship, a fully reusable super heavy-lift launch vehicle intended for missions to Mars and beyond.

In 2004, Elon Musk joined Tesla Motors (now Tesla, Inc.) as chairman of the board and lead investor, contributing $6.5 million to the company's Series A funding round. Tesla was originally founded by Martin Eberhard and Marc Tarpenning in 2003. Musk became CEO of Tesla in 2008. Tesla's first car, the Roadster, was launched in 2008 and was the first highway-legal electric car to use lithium-ion battery cells. Tesla later released the Model S in 2012, the Model X in 2015, the Model 3 in 2017, and the Model Y in 2020. Tesla is headquartered in Austin, Texas, and has Gigafactories in Nevada, Shanghai, Berlin, and Texas.

In 2006, Musk helped create SolarCity, a solar energy services company, along with his cousins Lyndon and Peter Rive. Tesla acquired SolarCity in 2016 for approximately $2.6 billion and integrated it into Tesla Energy. Tesla Energy produces the Powerwall home battery, Powerpack, and Megapack for energy storage.

In 2016, Elon Musk co-founded Neuralink, a neurotechnology company focused on developing brain-computer interface devices. The company aims to help people with neurological conditions and eventually enhance human cognitive abilities. Neuralink is headquartered in Fremont, California.

In 2016, Musk also founded The Boring Company, a tunnel construction and infrastructure company. The Boring Company built the Las Vegas Convention Center Loop, an underground transportation system using Tesla vehicles. The company is headquartered in Austin, Texas.

In 2023, Musk founded xAI, an artificial intelligence company focused on understanding the true nature of the universe. xAI developed Grok, an AI chatbot integrated with the X platform. xAI is headquartered in the San Francisco Bay Area.

In 2022, Elon Musk acquired Twitter for $44 billion and rebranded it to X in 2023. The platform is headquartered in San Francisco, California. Musk serves as the owner and CTO of X.

Elon Musk has received numerous awards and recognitions. He was named Time magazine's Person of the Year in 2021. Forbes has consistently ranked him among the wealthiest people in the world. He became the richest person in the world in January 2021.

Musk is known for his ambitious vision of making humanity a multi-planetary species through SpaceX, accelerating the transition to sustainable energy through Tesla, and advancing artificial intelligence through xAI. He is one of the most influential entrepreneurs and innovators of the 21st century.
